🔍 Understanding HRP and HERC Portfolio Optimization

📘 What is HRP (Hierarchical Risk Parity)?

HRP is a modern portfolio allocation technique introduced by Marcos López de Prado. It solves some of the critical problems with traditional optimization models like mean-variance, especially instability due to matrix inversion.

Key Steps in HRP
  • 1. Compute the correlation matrix
  • 2. Convert to distance matrix: \( d_{i,j} = \sqrt{0.5 \cdot (1 - \rho_{i,j})} \)
  • 3. Apply hierarchical clustering (e.g., Ward’s method)
  • 4. Quasi-diagonalization: Reorders the covariance matrix
  • 5. Recursive bisection: Allocates weights recursively
Advantages of HRP
  • No need to invert the covariance matrix
  • More robust to outliers and noise
  • Handles large portfolios better

📘 What is HERC (Hierarchical Equal Risk Contribution)?

HERC is an extension of HRP that incorporates the concept of Equal Risk Contribution into the allocation step. Instead of equal split, HERC ensures each cluster contributes equally to the total portfolio risk.

Key Steps in HERC
  • Follows same first 4 steps as HRP
  • Distributes risk contribution evenly across sub-clusters
  • Uses volatility or other risk metrics
Advantages of HERC
  • Allocates based on actual risk contributions
  • Improves diversification when volatilities differ
  • Handles heterogeneity in asset behavior
